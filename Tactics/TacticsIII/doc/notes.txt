Line intersection for attacks.

Straight line first.
Parabola is a straight line (perpendiclar to gravity) a vector to span th eplane it is in (0,0,1) and a 3 coefficients along the line vector (every vector is a one)
The origin of the line is at the apex of the parabola .

Anyway line:
Line is an origin and vector.
For a line segment the vector spans the length of the line and the segment is [0...1]
For a half line the vector is unit (or speed or whatever) and the segment is [0...inf]
for a line the range is [-inf to inf ]

Each grid is an axis aligned box. The line starts at the character's shoot height in its grid on one of the 1-3 planes facing the target. (depends on the vector to the target. Any non 0 is a direction
The length of the line is the total fall off range (basically range + damage).

A list is computed with grid coordinates and the range of the line that goes through it.
This is done with a configurable epsilon so diagonal lines don't clip every tiny corner. They are a bit rounded for hitting terrain.
For every transition then the height is computed.
Then for each grid the height range in and out is computed and matched to the materials at those levels.
Along the line the remaining damage of the attack is computed depending on the density and whether fire or poison is picked up.
Also obstacles/creatures are checked and the damage applied.

To compute the grids and the range that the line goes through, the scan starts at the origin of the line and the exiting face.
The neighbour at that face is selected.
The intersection with the line and all (other) faces of that line is computed.
Each face is an axis aligned plane
This is a new type of geometry that consists of:
- one float offset
- one axis aligned direction vector (X,Y,Z, or negative), stored as a Direction
Computing intersection with a line and this plane is relatively easy and fast math. It's just the delta along the axis between origin and offset divided by the vector's coefficient along that axis (if 0 then inf/-inf)
The nearest plane is found that is at least the epsilon away
The neighbour grid at that plane is taken next.
If the distance between the previous and the next plane is smaller than that epsilon, a corner is clipped and that
grid is skipped. Instead the epsilon is added to the next grid (or divided between previous and next?)
